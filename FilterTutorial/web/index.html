High Pass Low Pass Band Pass

High Pass

Audio filter that allows only certain high frequencies to pass through after a cut-off point. With a high-pass filter the lower frequencies will be cut off only allowing the upper ranges to pass. These filters are often used in audio mixing to roll off the low frequencies on more upper range instruments such as guitars, horns, and vocal to allow them to stand out in a mix and not bog down the more bass prominent regions of a song.

Low Pass

Audio filter that only allows frequencies on the lower end of an audio wave to pass through. The higher frequencies will be cut off allowing for a more prominent bass sound to be heard. This is often used in mixing on bass drums, toms, and bass guitars to allow for the most rounded and large sound possible.

Band Pass

Audio filter used to bypass both high and low frequencies only allowing for the midrange to pass. While high and low pass filters are used to give more bass and treble based instruments prominent in an audio mix, band-pass is used as more of a tool to make room for secondary instruments in the mix. This is done to avoid an overlap in frequencies so all instruments are heard at a audible level.
